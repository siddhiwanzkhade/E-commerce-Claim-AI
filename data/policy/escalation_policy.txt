Escalation Policy

A claim should be escalated when it requires human review, supervisor approval, or fraud/risk evaluation.

Escalation is required when:
- Customer urgency is high.
- Customer sentiment is highly negative.
- The order value is high.
- The customer has high previous-claim risk.
- The image evidence conflicts with the complaint.
- The order is outside the return or replacement window.
- The policy evidence is unclear.
- The claim involves repeated refund or replacement requests.

Manual review is recommended when:
- The model confidence is low.
- The vision model cannot clearly identify damage.
- The complaint is vague or incomplete.
- The delivery status conflicts with the customer complaint.
- The customer requests a refund for a delivered product without clear evidence.

Escalated claims should include:
- Complaint summary.
- Order metadata.
- Image evidence summary.
- Retrieved policy evidence.
- Recommended action and reason.
